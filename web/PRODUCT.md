# Product

Audience: internal human and agent maintainers shaping the public deck/site.
This is a public-safe product brief, not end-user documentation.

## Register

- **Project name:** Recursive Coding Agents
- **Format:** public talk deck and companion website
- **Canonical site:** https://recursivecodingagents.com
- **Speaker/author:** Raymond Weitekamp (OpenProse / RAW.works)

## Users

The primary audience is AI Engineer World's Fair attendees, coding-agent builders, and technically serious operators who already believe agents are powerful but need a sharper model for reliability. They encounter the project as a public talk deck and companion website, often before deciding whether to inspect OpenProse, ypi, RAW.works writing, or the linked RLM research.

## Product Purpose

This deck makes "recursive coding agents" legible and credible. It argues that the bottleneck is not raw model intelligence, but the management layer: specification, delegation, memory, verification, and structured test-time compute. Success looks like a reader or audience member understanding the bridge from Recursive Language Models to recursive coding agents, trusting that the claim is grounded in evidence, and knowing where to go next: OpenProse, ypi, RAW.works, and the referenced repositories.

## Brand Personality

Rigorous, pragmatic, provocative. The voice should feel confident without hype: opinionated enough to name the failure mode, careful enough to show evidence, and practical enough to turn the idea into tools people can inspect.

## Anti-references

Avoid generic AI SaaS gradients, template card grids, academic wall-of-text slides, and hacker-terminal cosplay. The talk should not look like a default startup landing page, a dense research PDF projected onto a stage, or a dark-mode developer toy that mistakes monospace styling for technical depth.

## Design Principles

1. **Evidence earns provocation.** Bold claims need visible support: cited papers, article previews, repository proof, and clear comparisons.
2. **One idea per slide.** Each viewport should carry a single claim or turn in the argument, with supporting detail kept scannable.
3. **Reliability is the thesis.** Every design move should reinforce trust: readable type, stable navigation, precise links, and verifiable artifacts.
4. **Show systems, not vibes.** Prefer diagrams, rubrics, repo previews, screenshots, and concrete examples over abstract AI-themed decoration.
5. **Stage clarity beats template polish.** This is a talk first and a website second; it must read instantly in a room and still hold up as a linked artifact afterward.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text and interactive elements. Preserve responsive slide layouts instead of shrinking a fixed canvas. Support keyboard navigation, real anchors, meaningful alt text for evidence imagery, and reduced-motion alternatives for scroll or hover motion.
